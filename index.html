About

ML, classical knowledge, and the space between.

I'm a second-year B.Tech student in AI/ML at Malla Reddy University, Hyderabad. My projects tend to sit somewhere between computation and culture — I've built a touchless Carnatic music interface, an n8n pipeline that fetches content from ancient scriptures based on user sentiment, and an ML system for electricity theft detection that hits 99.8% precision.

I care about the theoretical and ethical dimensions of what I build. Not as an afterthought — it's usually where the interesting problem is. If AI is going to touch human knowledge and creativity, it should do that carefully.

Outside of code I play flute, piano, and guitar, sing, and am teaching myself music production. Most of my technical projects are secretly music projects in disguise.

MudraLaya

Live · HCI + Music

A real-time, touchless Carnatic music interface that transforms hand gestures into musical notes across 11 ragas and 4 instrument timbres. Uses a rule-based expert system encoded in JSON to validate Swara transitions against classical raga structures — ensuring the output is musically coherent, not just sonically reactive.

React MediaPipe Hands Tone.js Firebase

ML Energy Theft Detection

ML · Complete

Benchmarked multiple ML classification models — Random Forest, XGBoost, Decision Tree — to detect anomalies in electricity consumption data indicative of theft. Random Forest achieved 99.84% precision, 99.23% recall, and 99.54% F1-score on the test set. Currently extending to multi-class classification on imbalanced datasets and building a front-end analytics dashboard.

Python Pandas scikit-learn XGBoost
